  • Case study — fintech client, anonymized

    Reply rates were stuck at 2.3%. Here's the audit that moved them.

    Every email described the product. None of them named the reader's Tuesday morning...

     →
    Real client engagement. Names and company withheld here out of discretion.

    A fintech client's CRO was sending outbound emails to prospects at other institutions, almost nothing came back. I audited the sequence and rewrote each underperforming email.

    01The body read like a brochure, not a diagnosis. Feature bullet points instead of one sentence naming the reader's actual operational pain in their own language.
    02The CTA explained what the sender got, not the reader. "I'd value the opportunity to share what we're seeing" answers nobody's question about why they should give up 20 minutes.
    ORIGINALSubject: Quick intro on our platform
    Hi [Name], I hope you're doing well. I'm reaching out as a fellow [shared alma mater] alum. I'm currently with [Company], where we've built a platform purpose-built for [industry]. Across the market, we consistently see teams managing this across multiple systems and spreadsheets. Our platform brings the full lifecycle into one place, with a focus on: - Driving efficiency through automation - Improving data integrity and controls - Providing real-time visibility for better decisions Would you be open to a brief 20-minute introduction in the coming days?
    REWRITESubject: [Shared alma mater] to [Firm], something relevant to your team
    Hi [Name], Reaching out as a fellow [shared alma mater] alum. Our founder built this platform specifically after watching teams like yours manage reporting across five disconnected systems. At your scale, that's not just slow, it's a real risk to data accuracy and decision speed. Worth a 20-minute call? I can share what we're seeing operationally across teams at your scale, useful either way.
    2.3% → 5.1%Reply rate, six weeks
    ↑Open rate, trending up

    What this means for Curate Group: the same discipline applies to ticketing and audience emails. If a send doesn't earn a click, the fix is almost always in the specific words, not a bigger list.
